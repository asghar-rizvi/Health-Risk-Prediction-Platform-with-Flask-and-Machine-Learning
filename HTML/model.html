models used for predictions

The Random Forest classifier, an ensemble learning method, is used to predict the likelihood of a heart attack. It combines multiple decision trees to improve accuracy, capturing complex patterns and interactions between risk factors.

The K-Neighbors classifier predicts liver disease by comparing a patient's health data with that of their nearest neighbors in the dataset. This non-parametric method is particularly useful for capturing local patterns and similarities in the data.

A Decision Tree classifier predicts the onset of diabetes by analyzing various health metrics. The tree structure allows for clear decision-making paths, making it easy to interpret and understand the factors contributing to diabetes.

Logistic Regression is applied to predict the presence of kidney disease. This model assesses the probability of disease occurrence based on linear relationships, making it effective for binary classification tasks.
